On the other hand, AppifyHub is a playground, of sorts. A sandbox. I tinker a lot with different app prototypes all the time, and I get tired of writing backend services for all of them... especially if the prototype is not good enough and it never makes it into the open. So, I came up with this. It's a collection of the most common, production-ready services for building apps that need backend support. Also, it's an awesome classroom for me until I get a hang of some new tech or framework that I'm trying out. ðŸ¤“

When I consider a prototype app to be "mature" enough (i.e. ready for a public release), I'll write a new backend for it, and I'll migrate the app to its new, standalone backend. But until then... these services are the app's backend.

If you got here because you know me personally, or follow me, I might have even given you access to the services to build prototypes of your own.

In that case, rest assured! ðŸ˜‰ All services:

  • are fully tested: unit, integration, manual
  • are self-contained: each runs in a separate Docker container
  • enable per-app access: an app can access only its resources, other apps are not accessible
  • are GDPR-compliant: I use app- and storage-level encryption, data is in EU, zero plain text
  • provide security out-of-the-box: I use REST with HTTPS as transport mechanism, with only one port open
  • are being open-sourced: I'm slowly going towards 100%, but I need more time
  • are easy to reconfigure: for each service I can add/remove new apps within minutes
  • are "minimal": (whatever that means) I use tech appropriate for the service's job and nothing more
